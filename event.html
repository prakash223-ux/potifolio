The Arulmigu Mutharamman Temple, located in Kulasekarapattinam, Thoothukudi district, Tamil Nadu, is an ancient and powerful shrine dedicated to Goddess Mutharamman and Lord Gnanamoorthiswarar. A unique feature is the combined, self-manifested idol (Swayambhu) of both deities in the sanctum sanctorum, a rarity in Hindu temples. The temple is most famous for its unique and vibrant 10 to 12-day Dussehra (Dasara) festival, the only one of its kind in Tamil Nadu. During the festival, millions of devotees observe a 41-day fast and dress up as various Hindu gods, goddesses, and epic characters, begging for alms which are then donated to the temple. The festival culminates in the dramatic enactment of the Goddess's victory over the demon Mahishasura on the seashore on the day of Vijayadashami. goddess acts as a guardian, swiftly responding to prayers for health and prosperity.
